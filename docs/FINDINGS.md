# Key findings

## Infrastructure expanded substantially

- The 2022 strategy listed 10 public PSA plants, 9 functional at the baseline.
- Public records identify at least 27 distinct facility sites with an installed or commissioned PSA plant by April 2026. This is a reconciled evidence minimum, not an official current-functionality inventory.
- Fifteen World Bank-supported mobile PSA plants were installed and commissioned at named hospitals across all ten provinces by project close in 2024.
- A separate Global Fund–UNOPS programme commissioned all six planned plants by April 2026.
- Kitwe Teaching Hospital's Sweden/UNICEF-supported plant is separate from those programmes.

## Supply methods diversified

- The national strategy baseline reported zero public facilities with bulk liquid oxygen.
- At least two hospital LOX installations were publicly identifiable by December 2024: Levy Mwanawasa and Livingstone.
- WHO supplied 4,000 pre-filled cylinders in 2022, valued at US$744,000.
- A reported 8,000-cylinder procurement in 2023 remains secondary-source evidence; no Global Fund primary record was found.

## Workforce systems grew

- A national programme trained 76 trainers and 672 additional nurses during 2023–2024.
- A formal oxygen-management and maintenance curriculum was in use at two colleges by December 2025.
- Earlier Unitaid-supported work reported staff training in pulse oximetry and oxygen at seven hospitals.

## Reliability and bedside access remained incomplete

- In the late-2023 assessment, 73% of assessed PSA plants operated year-round and 64% operated 24 hours per day.
- Sixty-six of 91 assessed hospitals lacked a bulk oxygen source.
- Twenty-one of 91 had medical-gas piping.
- Forty-two of 91 lacked an available biomedical engineer or technician.
- Functional pulse-oximetry access averaged approximately one device per ten beds.

## National strategy achievement cannot be fully scored publicly

The public record verifies many projects and outputs but does not publish the national percentage of public hospitals with functional oxygen systems, the central 60% strategy goal. The completed National Health Strategic Plan midterm review is the strongest missing document to request.

## Most important missing outcome

No public national annual measure was found for the percentage of patients prescribed oxygen who received safe oxygen continuously without interruption.
